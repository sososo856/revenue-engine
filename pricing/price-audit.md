## Audit Findings & Recommendations

### 1. Starter Website — $997 + $97/mo
**Verdict: UNDERPRICED**

**Why:** A custom 5-page website with mobile optimization, speed optimization, lead capture, and ongoing hosting/maintenance for under $1,000 is below market rate. Comparable agencies charge $2,500-5,000 for similar scope. The $97/mo recurring is fair but the setup fee leaves money on the table.

**Recommendation:** Raise to **$1,497 setup + $127/mo**

**Justification:** At $1,497, you're still well below competitors while signaling higher quality. The $127/mo is a $360/year increase per client that compounds meaningfully at scale. Existing clients stay at their original rate. New clients won't notice the difference — the value proposition (fast, mobile, leads) remains the same.

**Impact:** 4 new Starter clients/month at $1,497 vs $997 = $2,000/month additional revenue = $24,000/year

---

### 2. Growth Website — $1,997 + $197/mo
**Verdict: SLIGHTLY UNDERPRICED**

**Why:** This is your bread and butter — custom site + FAQ chatbot + LeadCatch + monthly optimization. The chatbot alone is worth $207/mo to standalone buyers. Bundling it into a $197/mo package that also includes hosting, optimization, and content updates undervalues the bot.

**Recommendation:** Raise to **$2,497 setup + $247/mo**

**Justification:** The Growth package delivers $8,000-12,000/month in estimated additional revenue for clients (based on chiropractor case study). Charging $2,497 setup is still under 1 month's ROI for most clients. The $247/mo is justified by the chatbot value alone.

**Impact:** At 2 Growth clients/month, the $500 setup increase + $50/mo recurring increase = $1,000 + $100/mo compounding = $13,200 additional in year one

---

### 3. Scale Website — $4,997 + $497/mo
**Verdict: PRICED APPROPRIATELY**

**Why:** This is the premium tier and the anchor for the pricing page. It's positioned correctly to make Growth look like a deal. The deliverables (landing pages, A/B testing, priority support, strategy calls) justify the premium.

**Recommendation:** Hold at current pricing but add one more deliverable to increase perceived value: **include a monthly Loom video walking through their analytics and recommendations** (takes 10 minutes, adds perceived value of $200+/month).

---

### 4. FAQ Chatbot (standalone) — $207/mo
**Verdict: UNDERPRICED**

**Why:** The chatbot generates $2,400-6,900/month in average additional revenue for clients (based on real case studies). At $207/mo, the ROI is 11-33x. Clients are getting extraordinary value. Additionally, $207 is an odd number that doesn't anchor to a standard pricing tier.

**Recommendation:** Raise to **$297/mo** for new standalone clients.

**Justification:** At $297/mo with $4,000+ average revenue generated, clients still get a 13x+ ROI. The $297 price point is standard SaaS pricing and feels more "real product" than $207. Existing clients keep their rate. Clients who get the chatbot as part of the Growth/Scale bundle keep the bundled rate.

**Impact:** 3 standalone bot clients/month at $297 vs $207 = $270/month additional = $3,240/year

---

### 5. LeadCatch (SaaS) — $47-197/mo
**Verdict: BOTTOM TIER UNDERPRICED, TOP TIER APPROPRIATE**

**Why:** The $47/mo tier is too cheap for a product that generates real leads. It attracts the most price-sensitive customers who also require the most support. The $197/mo tier is reasonable for the features included.

**Recommendation:**
- Kill the $47 tier. Lowest tier should be **$97/mo**
- Keep mid-tier at $147/mo
- Keep top tier at $197/mo
- Add an **agency tier at $497/mo** (white-label, install on unlimited client sites)

**Justification:** The $47 customer costs as much in support as the $197 customer but generates 75% less revenue. Raising the floor to $97 filters for better customers and increases LTV.

**Impact:** Depends on volume, but eliminating the $47 tier and converting those users to $97 could increase SaaS MRR by 50-100%.

---

### 6. Cold Outreach Vault — $47
**Verdict: CORRECTLY PRICED (for now)**

**Why:** $47 is the right impulse-buy price point for a digital product. It's designed as a low-friction entry to your ecosystem, not a primary revenue driver.

**Recommendation:** Hold at $47 but create an **"Agency Edition" at $197** that includes additional templates, the Notion CRM template, and 3 recorded Loom audit examples. This captures the higher-willingness-to-pay segment without raising the base price.

---

### 7. Free Website Audit — $0
**Verdict: CORRECT — but add a paid upgrade**

**Why:** The free audit is a conversion tool, not a product. It should stay free. But there's a missed monetization opportunity for prospects who want more depth.

**Recommendation:** Keep the 5-point audit free. Add a **$197 "Deep Dive Audit"** that includes:
- Full PageSpeed analysis with specific fix recommendations
- Competitor comparison (top 3 competitors scored)
- Loom walkthrough (15 minutes, not 90 seconds)
- Written report with prioritized action items
- 30-minute strategy call to discuss findings
- Upsell path to website rebuild (credit the $197 toward any package)

This turns the audit from pure lead gen into a revenue line while maintaining the free version as the entry point.
