# Podcast Pitch — Template for Sub-5K Download Podcasts
# 5 podcast targets + pitch + what to offer
# Updated: April 2026

---

## Why Small Podcasts

- Sub-5K download podcasts are hungry for guests (less gatekeeping)
- The audience is niche and engaged (higher trust per listener)
- The host often becomes a referral partner
- Each episode lives forever as content (long-tail SEO + social clips)

---

## 5 Podcast Categories to Target

### 1. Local Business Marketing Podcasts
**Search for:** "local business marketing podcast," "small business marketing show"
**Why:** Direct ICP. Listeners are local business owners looking for marketing help.
**Your angle:** "How I help local businesses get 3-5x more leads from their websites using AI chatbots and smart lead capture."

### 2. Young Entrepreneur / Teen Business Podcasts
**Search for:** "young entrepreneur podcast," "teen business podcast," "Gen Z business"
**Why:** Your age is a unique hook. Hosts love the story of a 17-year-old running an agency and building SaaS.
**Your angle:** "How I built a web agency and SaaS product while still in high school — what I've learned about pricing, sales, and shipping fast."

### 3. Agency Growth Podcasts
**Search for:** "web agency podcast," "agency growth show," "freelance to agency"
**Why:** Agency owners are potential white-label partners and can learn from your systems.
**Your angle:** "How I built a productized agency at 17 — systems, automation, and packaging services for recurring revenue."

### 4. AI/No-Code Business Podcasts
**Search for:** "AI business podcast," "no-code podcast," "build with AI"
**Why:** Tech-savvy audience interested in AI applications for business.
**Your angle:** "Building AI chatbots for local businesses — how FAQ agents generate $2,000+/month in revenue for a $297/month tool."

### 5. Side Hustle / Indie Maker Podcasts
**Search for:** "side hustle podcast," "indie hackers podcast," "build in public"
**Why:** Aspiring builders who might use LeadCatch or refer clients to you.
**Your angle:** "How I turned repetitive agency work into a SaaS product — the LeadCatch story."

## The Pitch Template

**Subject:** Guest pitch: 17-year-old running a web agency + SaaS [for [Podcast Name]]

Hi [Host Name],

I'm Dan — 17 years old, running a web agency and building a SaaS product called LeadCatch. I help local businesses (dentists, plumbers, gyms, etc.) get more leads from their websites using fast custom sites, smart lead capture, and AI chatbots.

I think your audience would find my story interesting because:

1. **I started from zero at 16** — no network, no credibility, no funding. Built a pipeline using personalized Loom audits sent to businesses with bad websites. 40% close rate on calls.

2. **I turned repetitive agency work into a product** — LeadCatch started because I kept building the same lead capture form for every client. Now it's a standalone SaaS with paying users.

3. **I have specific, tactical insights** — I've audited 200+ local business websites, sent 500+ cold emails, and have real data on what actually moves the needle for small businesses online.

**Topics I could cover on your show:**
- How to land your first agency clients with $0 marketing budget
- Why most local business websites fail (and the 5 things that fix them)
- Building an FAQ chatbot business targeting local services
- How to price agency services (I went from $500/project to $5,000 packages)
- The tech stack that runs my agency for $80/month

**What I can offer your audience:**
- Free website audit for any listener (I'll record a personalized Loom)
- Discount code for LeadCatch
- My cold email template pack (the one that generated 11 clients from 500 emails)

I keep episodes tight, tactical, and story-driven. No fluff. Happy to match whatever format works best for your show.

Here's some of my content so you can get a feel for my style: [LinkedIn profile link]

Thanks for considering it, [Host Name]. Big fan of the show.

— Dan
[Website URL]

## What to Offer in Exchange

**For the audience (value for listeners):**
- Free website audit (record Loom for anyone who emails you after the episode)
- LeadCatch discount code (30% off first 3 months)
- Free download: Cold Email Template Pack or Website Conversion Checklist
- Direct email for questions (builds your list)

**For the host (value for the podcast):**
- Share the episode across your channels (LinkedIn, X, email list)
- Write a LinkedIn post specifically about the episode and tag the host
- Offer to be a resource for future episodes
- If they run a business, offer a free audit of their site

---

## Pre-Episode Preparation

- Listen to 3-5 recent episodes to understand format, tone, and typical questions
- Prepare 3-5 specific stories with numbers (cold email results, client case studies, pricing evolution)
- Have your talking points ready but don't script — conversational is better
- Test your mic and internet before recording
- Have your CTA ready: "If you want a free audit of your website, email me at [email]. I'll record a personalized video for any listener."

---

## Post-Episode Promotion

- [ ] Share on LinkedIn (tag the host, share one key insight from the episode)
- [ ] Share on X/Twitter (clip the best 60-second moment if video, or quote + link)
- [ ] Email your list: "I was on [Podcast Name] talking about [topic] — here's the episode"
- [ ] Add to your website: "As featured on [Podcast Name]"
- [ ] DM the host: "Episode is live! Just shared it across all my channels. Thanks again for having me."
- [ ] Comment on the episode's social post to boost engagement
