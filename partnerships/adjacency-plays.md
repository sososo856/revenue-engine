# Adjacency Plays — 5 Products Serving Same ICP, Different Needs
# Cross-promotion proposals for each
# Updated: April 2026

---

## The Strategy

Find products that serve local business owners but solve a different problem than you do. Propose cross-promotion: you recommend them, they recommend you. Both audiences benefit without competition.

---

## Play 1: Local SEO Tool (e.g., BrightLocal, Whitespark, or smaller indie tool)

**What they do:** Help local businesses rank higher on Google Maps and local search results.
**What you do:** Help local businesses convert that traffic once it reaches their site.
**The overlap:** Same customer (local business owner). Different stage of the funnel (they drive traffic, you convert it).

**Cross-Promotion Proposal:**

"Hey [Founder/Marketing Lead],

I run a web agency and a lead capture tool (LeadCatch) for local businesses. We have a natural synergy:

You help businesses get found on Google. We help them convert that traffic into leads once they land on the site.

Proposal:
- I recommend [Their Tool] to every client as part of our SEO recommendations (we work with 15+ active local businesses)
- You recommend LeadCatch or our agency to businesses whose sites aren't converting despite good rankings

This could be as simple as:
- A 'Recommended Tools' section on each other's sites
- A mention in each other's newsletters/emails
- Co-created content: 'How to Rank AND Convert: The Complete Local Business Playbook'

Interested in exploring this? I think both our audiences would benefit.

— Dan"

---

## Play 2: Reputation Management Tool (e.g., Podium, Birdeye, GatherUp, or indie alternative)

**What they do:** Help businesses collect, manage, and respond to online reviews.
**What you do:** Display those reviews on the website and capture leads.
**The overlap:** Same customer. Reviews are a trust signal you both need.

**Cross-Promotion Proposal:**

"Hey [Name],

I build lead-generating websites for local businesses and one of the first things I do is put Google reviews front-and-center on every site.

The problem: many of my clients don't have enough reviews, or they're not actively collecting them.

That's where you come in.

Proposal:
- When I build a client's website, I recommend [Their Tool] for review collection and management
- When your customers need a website that actually displays and leverages those reviews for conversions, you send them to me

We could also co-create: 'The Ultimate Guide to Reviews: Collecting, Displaying, and Converting' — free resource we both distribute.

The math works for both of us. Your tool collects reviews. My sites display them. Client gets more reviews AND more leads.

Worth a chat?

— Dan"

---

## Play 3: Appointment Scheduling Tool (e.g., Acuity, Square Appointments, Setmore)

**What they do:** Help service businesses manage bookings and scheduling.
**What you do:** Capture leads and drive them to book.
**The overlap:** Same customer. Both involved in the customer journey.

**Cross-Promotion Proposal:**

"Hey [Name],

I integrate appointment scheduling into every website I build for local businesses — dentists, salons, chiropractors, etc.

I'm currently using [calendar tool], but I've been looking at [Their Tool] and I think it could be a better fit for my clients because [specific reason — better UI, better for their niche, etc.].

Proposal:
- I recommend and integrate [Their Tool] into every client website (15+ active clients, growing)
- In exchange, [Their Tool] recommends my agency or LeadCatch to customers who need a better website to drive bookings

We could also:
- Create a setup guide: 'How to Connect [Their Tool] with LeadCatch for Maximum Bookings'
- Offer a joint bundle discount for mutual customers
- Feature each other in our respective email newsletters

Both our tools make the other more valuable. Your scheduling tool converts better with LeadCatch's lead capture. LeadCatch becomes stickier with your booking integration.

Open to discussing?

— Dan"

## Play 4: Email Marketing for Local Businesses (e.g., Mailchimp, Constant Contact, Brevo competitor, or niche tool)

**What they do:** Help businesses send marketing emails, newsletters, and promotions.
**What you do:** Capture the leads that go into those email lists.
**The overlap:** Same customer. Lead capture feeds email marketing.

**Cross-Promotion Proposal:**

"Hey [Name],

Interesting angle I've been thinking about: many of my agency clients struggle with email marketing because they don't have enough leads on their list. Meanwhile, your customers probably have the email tool but struggle to grow their list.

LeadCatch captures leads from websites 24/7 — those leads flow directly into the client's contact list. If those leads landed in [Their Tool] automatically, the client gets a growing email list without lifting a finger.

Proposal:
- Native integration: LeadCatch leads auto-sync to [Their Tool] contact lists
- Co-marketing: 'The Local Business Growth Stack: Capture Leads + Nurture with Email' — joint webinar or guide
- Mutual referrals: I recommend [Their Tool] for email. You recommend LeadCatch for lead capture.

The integration alone would make both products stickier. Business owner captures a lead on Monday, and by Tuesday they're getting an automated email welcome sequence from [Their Tool].

Want to explore this?

— Dan"

## Play 5: Social Media Management for Local Businesses (e.g., Later, Buffer, Hootsuite, or niche local tool)

**What they do:** Help businesses schedule and manage social media posts.
**What you do:** Convert the traffic that social media drives to their website.
**The overlap:** Same customer. Social drives traffic, website converts it.

**Cross-Promotion Proposal:**

"Hey [Name],

I keep seeing the same pattern with local businesses: they post great content on social media, drive traffic to their website... and the website doesn't convert.

They think social media doesn't work. In reality, social media worked perfectly — the website just dropped the ball.

I build websites and lead capture tools that fix that last step.

Proposal:
- Content piece: 'Why Your Social Media Traffic Isn't Converting (And How to Fix It)' — I write it, you distribute it, we both get credited
- Mutual newsletter features: I mention [Their Tool] as the best way to manage social. You mention LeadCatch as the best way to capture social traffic.
- Joint customer offer: Businesses using both tools get a discount (10-15% off each)

Social drives the traffic. LeadCatch captures the lead. [Their Tool] helps create the next post about the result. Virtuous cycle.

Interested?

— Dan"

## Execution Checklist

For each adjacency play:

- [ ] Identify the specific company/product to approach
- [ ] Find the right person to contact (founder for small companies, partnerships lead for larger ones)
- [ ] Personalize the proposal (mention specific features, overlap, numbers)
- [ ] Send the pitch
- [ ] Follow up in 5 days if no response
- [ ] If they're interested, propose a 30-minute call to flesh out the details
- [ ] Start with the simplest version (newsletter mention) and build from there
- [ ] Track results and expand what works

---

## Priority Order

1. **Appointment Scheduling** — Most natural integration, direct revenue impact for clients
2. **Reputation Management** — Reviews + websites is a killer combo for local businesses
3. **Email Marketing** — Lead capture → email list is a clear pipeline
4. **Local SEO** — Traffic + conversion is the full picture
5. **Social Media** — Furthest from core but large potential audience
