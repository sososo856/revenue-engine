# Client Check-In Messages — 5 Genuine Check-Ins (Not Pitches)
# For past and current clients, designed to maintain relationships
# Updated: April 2026

---

## Message 1: The "How's Business" Check-In

**Best for:** Clients you haven't spoken to in 2-3 months.

**Subject:** How are things going, [First Name]?

Hi [First Name],

Been a few months since we last connected — wanted to check in and see how things are going with [Business Name].

How's business been this quarter? Any big wins or challenges I should know about?

No agenda here — just genuinely curious how things are going and whether the site is working the way you need it to.

If anything comes up that I can help with, even just a quick question, don't hesitate to reach out.

Hope you're doing well.

— Dan

---

## Message 2: The "I Saw This and Thought of You" Check-In

**Best for:** When you come across something relevant to their business.

**Subject:** Saw this and thought of you

Hi [First Name],

Was reading an article about [relevant topic — examples below] and immediately thought of [Business Name]:

- **For a dentist:** "The latest patient acquisition trends for 2026 — apparently online booking is now the #1 factor patients consider when choosing a new dentist."
- **For HVAC:** "A report just came out about how homeowners choose emergency service providers. Speed of website and click-to-call are the top 2 factors."
- **For a gym:** "Interesting data on gym sign-ups — turns out 70% of people who visit a gym's website do it between 8pm-midnight."
- **For a restaurant:** "New data on how diners choose restaurants — Google Business Profile photos are now the #1 factor, ahead of reviews."
- **For a law firm:** "Interesting study on how people choose attorneys — FAQ pages that answer 'how much does it cost' get 3x more conversions than those that don't."

[Link to article or mention where you saw it]

Thought you'd find it interesting. No other reason for reaching out — just staying curious about your industry.

Hope things are great with you and the team.

— Dan

## Message 3: The Celebration Check-In

**Best for:** When you notice something positive about their business (new review, anniversary, award, expansion).

**Subject:** Congrats, [First Name]!

Hi [First Name],

Just saw [specific thing you noticed — examples below]:

- "You hit 100 Google reviews — that's a huge milestone. The social proof is seriously powerful."
- "Noticed you're expanding to a second location — that's amazing. Congrats!"
- "[Business Name] just got featured in [local publication]. Well deserved."
- "Saw you just celebrated your [X]-year anniversary. That's no small feat in this economy."
- "Your recent post about [topic] was really well done. Great content."

Just wanted to say congrats. That kind of growth doesn't happen by accident — it's a reflection of the work you and your team put in.

No other reason for reaching out. Just genuinely happy to see [Business Name] doing well.

— Dan

## Message 4: The "Useful Tip" Check-In

**Best for:** Adding value without any ask. Send after 4-6 months of no contact.

**Subject:** Quick tip that might help [Business Name]

Hi [First Name],

Had a random thought about [Business Name] and wanted to share a quick tip:

[Choose the tip most relevant to their situation:]

**Option A (if they run Google Ads):** "If you're still running Google Ads, make sure your landing page headline matches your ad headline exactly. I've seen this one change improve conversion rates by 20-30% — and it takes 5 minutes."

**Option B (if they get good reviews):** "You have [X] Google reviews now — have you thought about adding them directly to your homepage? Businesses that display reviews on-site see a significant bump in conversions. I can send you a quick tutorial if you want."

**Option C (if their site has been up 6+ months):** "Your site has been live for [X] months now — it might be worth checking if your images are still loading fast and your form is working on the latest iOS update. Things break quietly over time."

**Option D (if a relevant season is approaching):** "With [season] coming up, it might be worth adding a seasonal banner or offer to your homepage. Even something simple like 'Spring Special: [offer]' can boost conversions during peak search periods."

No ask. Just thought of you and wanted to share.

Hope things are going well.

— Dan

## Message 5: The Holiday/End-of-Year Check-In

**Best for:** December/January or any natural reset point.

**Subject:** Thanks for a great year, [First Name]

Hi [First Name],

As the year wraps up, I wanted to take a second to say thanks.

Working with [Business Name] has been one of the highlights of my year. Your trust means a lot to me, and I'm proud of what we've built together — [specific result, e.g., "the site has generated over 200 leads since launch" or "your online presence is in a completely different place than where we started"].

Looking ahead to next year, if there's anything you'd like to change, improve, or add to your website — or just any way I can support [Business Name] — I'm here.

Wishing you and the team a great holiday season and a strong start to the new year.

Grateful for you,
Dan

## When to Send Each

| Message | Trigger | Frequency |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Message 1 | 2-3 months of no contact | Once per quarter max |
| Message 2 | You find something relevant | Anytime (max once/month) |
| Message 3 | You notice a positive event | Immediately when noticed |
| Message 4 | 4-6 months of no contact | Once, then wait for reply |
| Message 5 | December/New Year | Once per year |

## Rules

1. These are NOT pitches. Do not include a CTA, upsell, or referral ask.
2. Send from your personal email tone, not a marketing template.
3. If they reply, have a genuine conversation. If it naturally leads to business, great. If not, also great.
4. The goal is to stay top-of-mind so that when they need something (or know someone who does), you're the first person they think of.
5. Keep a log of when you last checked in with each client so you don't over-communicate or under-communicate.
