# Breakup Emails — Final Attempts for 30+ Day No-Reply Leads
# The "last message" that often gets the reply
# Updated: April 2026

---

## Why Breakup Emails Work

The breakup email creates psychological urgency through loss aversion. When someone realizes they're about to lose access to you, they often respond — even if they've ignored everything else. These should feel genuine, not manipulative. You're simply being respectful of their time by not continuing to follow up.

---

## Template 1: The Honest Closing (Best all-around)

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about [Business Name]'s website and haven't heard back. No worries at all — I know you're busy running a business.

I don't want to keep filling your inbox if this isn't relevant, so I'll assume the timing isn't right and close out my notes on this.

If anything changes down the road — whether it's 2 months or 2 years — I'm always happy to take a fresh look. Just reply to this email and I'll pick it right back up.

Wishing you a great rest of the year.

— Dan
[Website URL]

## Template 2: The Quick Poll (Gets replies because it's easy to answer)

**Subject:** Quick question (1 second to answer)

Hi [First Name],

I've followed up a few times and want to respect your time. Usually when I don't hear back, it's one of three reasons:

(a) You're interested but the timing is wrong — happy to circle back later
(b) You've already solved this — great, no need for me
(c) You saw my emails and thought "not for me" — totally fair

Which one is it? Just reply with the letter. Takes 1 second and helps me know whether to follow up or move on.

Thanks either way,
Dan

## Sending Rules

1. **Only send ONE breakup email.** Don't send multiple "final" messages — it kills credibility.

2. **Pick the template that matches the situation:**
   - Template 1 — Default. Works for everyone.
   - Template 2 — When you want a reply more than a sale.
   - Template 3 — When you want to leave them with something useful.
   - Template 4 — When you've actually noticed competitor activity.
   - Template 5 — When you have a recent win in their industry.
   - Template 6 — When a relevant season is approaching.
   - Template 7 — When the lead seems like a dead end but might know people.

3. **After sending the breakup email:**
   - Mark the lead as "Breakup Sent" in your CRM
   - Set a 90-day reminder to revisit with the warm reactivation sequence
   - Do NOT follow up again until the 90-day mark unless they reply

4. **If they reply to the breakup:** Respond within 2 hours. The breakup often triggers urgency — don't waste it by being slow.

5. **Track your breakup reply rate.** A well-written breakup email should get a 5-10% reply rate. If it's lower, test a different template.
