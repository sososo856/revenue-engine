**Tweet 2:**
Why sites are slow (it's almost always the same reasons):

- Uncompressed images (a single photo can be 5MB)
- Too many plugins/scripts
- Cheap shared hosting
- No caching
- No CDN

The #1 culprit? A restaurant with 47 full-resolution photos. Load time: 14.3 seconds.

**Tweet 3:**
Free fixes anyone can do today:

1. Compress images at squoosh.app (saves 60-80% file size, no visible quality loss)
2. Remove plugins you don't use
3. Enable browser caching (your host probably has a toggle for this)
4. Sign up for Cloudflare free tier (instant CDN)

This alone can cut load time in half.

**Tweet 4:**
If you want to go further:

5. Lazy-load images below the fold
6. Minify CSS and JavaScript
7. Upgrade hosting (Vercel, Netlify, or a VPS > GoDaddy shared)
8. Use WebP image format instead of PNG/JPG

Getting under 2 seconds is totally achievable.

**Tweet 5:**
The revenue impact is real.

Every 1-second improvement in load time increases conversions by ~7%.

If you go from 6 seconds to 2 seconds — that's a 28% increase in conversions.

On 1,000 monthly visitors converting at 2%, that's 5-6 extra leads/month from speed alone.

**Tweet 6:**
Test your site right now: pagespeed.web.dev

If your mobile score is under 50, you're losing money daily.
