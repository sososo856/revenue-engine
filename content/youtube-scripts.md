## Script 1: "I Audited 50 Local Business Websites. Here's What I Found."

- Broken mobile layouts — show on phone side-by-side with desktop
- No clear CTA — scroll through a homepage and ask "what am I supposed to do here?"
- Generic contact forms — show a 9-field form nobody would fill out

Use a scoring system: rate each site 1-10 and tally the results.

**Solution (3:00-7:00):**
Walk through each fix with screen recordings:

Fix 1 — Speed: "Go to squoosh.app, compress every image. Switch to a faster host. Enable caching. This alone takes most sites from 6 seconds to under 3."

Fix 2 — Mobile: "Open your site on your phone right now. If you have to pinch to zoom or scroll sideways, your site is losing 70% of your visitors."

Fix 3 — CTA: "Put one big button above the fold. 'Get a Free Quote' or 'Book Now.' Make it impossible to miss."

Fix 4 — Forms: "Cut your form to 3 fields max. Name, phone, what do you need. Every extra field costs you 10-15% of submissions."

Fix 5 — Social proof: "Pull your best Google reviews and put them on your homepage. Real names. Star ratings. Visible without scrolling."

## Script 9: "How to Price Your Services (Stop Undercharging)"

**Hook (0:00-0:25):**

**Problem (0:25-2:30):**
- The undercharging epidemic: especially for young/new freelancers
- Fear-based pricing: "What if they say no?"
- The hourly trap: trading time for money with no leverage
- How underpricing attracts the worst clients (most demanding, least grateful)

**Solution (2:30-7:30):**
Framework 1 — Value-based pricing: "Don't price based on hours. Price based on value. If a website generates $5,000/month in leads for a dentist, charging $2,000 for that website is a steal. The ROI frames the conversation."

Framework 2 — Tiered packages: "Three tiers. Starter, Growth, Scale. The top tier makes the middle feel reasonable. 80% pick the middle. Show the actual pricing page."

Framework 3 — Recurring revenue: "Add monthly maintenance, optimization, and reporting. $97-497/month. 12 clients at $197/month = $2,364/month before any new projects."

Framework 4 — Price increases: "Raise prices every 2-3 months. Grandfather existing clients. New clients pay the new rate. If nobody says your price is too high, you're too cheap."

The mindset shift: "Your price is a filter. Low prices attract price-sensitive clients who will nickel-and-dime you. Higher prices attract clients who value results."

**CTA (7:30-8:15):**
"If you're freelancing and feel underpaid, the fix isn't more clients — it's better pricing. Take 30 minutes today and restructure your offers into 3 tiers. You'll close the same number of deals at 2-3x the price. If you want me to review your pricing, drop it in the comments — I'll give you honest feedback."
