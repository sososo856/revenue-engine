# Pain Point Survey — Send to 30 Past Leads/Clients
# Questions designed to sharpen positioning
# Updated: April 2026

---

## Purpose

This survey goes to past leads (both won and lost) and current clients. The goal is to hear their words so you can use their exact language in your marketing, positioning, and sales conversations. People buy when they feel understood — this survey gives you the words to make them feel understood.

---

## Who to Send It To

**Group 1: Current clients (10 people)**
They know you, trust you, and will give honest feedback about what made them buy.

**Group 2: Won clients who churned (5 people)**
They bought but left. Their feedback reveals retention issues.

**Group 3: Leads who didn't buy (10 people)**
They were interested but didn't convert. Their feedback reveals sales issues.

**Group 4: Leads who said "not now" (5 people)**
They might buy eventually. Their feedback reveals timing and positioning issues.

## The Questions

### Survey: Help Dan Build Better

*This takes 3 minutes. Your honest answers help me serve local businesses better.*

---

**Question 1: The Problem (Open-ended)**

"When you first started looking for help with your website or online presence, what was the #1 problem or frustration that motivated you to look?"

*Why this question matters: Reveals the trigger event and the language prospects use to describe their pain. Use these exact words in your marketing.*

---

**Question 2: The Alternatives (Open-ended)**

"Before reaching out to me (or considering my services), what had you already tried to fix the problem? What worked and what didn't?"

*Why this question matters: Reveals competitor landscape and failed solutions. Use this to position yourself against alternatives.*

---

**Question 3: The Decision Factor (Multiple choice + open)**

"What was the MOST important factor in deciding whether to work with someone on your website?"

- [ ] Price
- [ ] Speed of delivery
- [ ] Proven results / case studies
- [ ] Personal connection / trust
- [ ] Guarantee or risk reduction
- [ ] Recommendation from someone I trust
- [ ] Other: _______________

*Why this question matters: Tells you what to emphasize in sales conversations and on your pricing page.*

---

**Question 4: The Hesitation (Open-ended)**

"What was your biggest hesitation or concern before deciding? What almost stopped you from moving forward?"

*Why this question matters: Reveals objections you need to address proactively in marketing and sales calls.*

---

**Question 5: The Result (Open-ended, for clients only)**

"If you worked with me, what's the most meaningful result or change you've experienced? If you didn't work with me, what result were you hoping for?"

*Why this question matters: Client words about results become your most powerful testimonials and ad copy.*

---

**Question 6: The Description (Open-ended)**

"If you were telling a friend about what I do, how would you describe it in one sentence?"

*Why this question matters: This is your positioning. If 15 people describe you the same way, that's your tagline.*

---

**Question 7: The Missing Piece (Open-ended)**

"Is there anything you wish I offered that I don't? Any service, feature, or support that would make working with me more valuable?"

*Why this question matters: Reveals product development opportunities and unmet needs in your market.*

---

**Question 8: The Price Perception (Scale + open)**

"How would you describe the value relative to the price?"

- [ ] Incredible value — way more than I paid for
- [ ] Good value — worth every penny
- [ ] Fair — about what I expected
- [ ] A bit expensive — but the results justified it
- [ ] Too expensive — didn't feel worth it

"Any thoughts on pricing you'd like to share?" (optional open field)

*Why this question matters: Tells you if you're underpriced (most responses are "incredible value" → raise prices) or if pricing is a barrier.*

## How to Build the Survey

**Option A: Google Forms (free, fast)**
Create a Google Form with the 8 questions. Share the link. Responses go to a Google Sheet automatically.

**Option B: Typeform/Tally (better experience)**
More visually appealing. Better completion rates. Tally.so is free and clean.

**Option C: Direct email replies**
For a small group, just send the questions in the email and ask them to reply. More personal, higher response rate, harder to aggregate.

## What to Do With the Responses

### Step 1: Read every response carefully (Day 1 after closing)

Highlight phrases that stand out — the exact language people use to describe their problems, hesitations, and results.

### Step 2: Build a language bank (Day 2)

Create a document with three sections:

**Pain phrases:** The exact words people use to describe their problems.
- Example: "My website was just sitting there doing nothing"
- Example: "I was tired of paying for ads and getting no calls"
- Example: "I knew my site was bad but I didn't know how to fix it"

**Hesitation phrases:** The exact words people use to describe their objections.
- Example: "I wasn't sure a website could actually generate leads"
- Example: "I'd been burned by a web designer before"
- Example: "I thought it would take months"

**Result phrases:** The exact words people use to describe outcomes.
- Example: "I finally feel like my online presence matches the quality of my business"
- Example: "We're getting calls from people we've never met"
- Example: "The chatbot pays for itself in the first week"

### Step 3: Update your marketing (Week 2)

Use the language bank to rewrite:
- [ ] Website headlines and copy
- [ ] LinkedIn post hooks
- [ ] Cold email subject lines and body copy
- [ ] Sales call talking points
- [ ] Proposal language
- [ ] Ad copy (if running ads)

### Step 4: Identify product opportunities (Week 2)

From Question 7 ("What do you wish I offered?"), look for patterns:
- If 5+ people mention the same thing → build it
- If 3-4 people mention it → add it to the roadmap
- If 1-2 people mention it → note it but don't act yet

### Step 5: Adjust pricing if needed (Week 3)

From Question 8 (price perception):
- If 70%+ say "incredible value" or "good value" → you're underpriced, raise by 20-30%
- If 50%+ say "fair" → you're priced correctly
- If 30%+ say "too expensive" → you have a value communication problem, not a price problem

## Follow-Up

After the survey closes:

1. Send a thank-you to everyone who responded: "Thank you for taking the time — your feedback is genuinely shaping how I build my business. [Deliver the promised incentive.]"

2. If a response reveals someone who's open to working with you again, follow up personally: "Your survey response mentioned [specific thing]. I actually have something that might help with that — want to chat?"

3. Share one aggregate insight publicly (LinkedIn/X): "I surveyed 30 local business owners about their website frustrations. The #1 answer surprised me: [insight]. Here's what I learned..."
