# Who to Refer — Post-Interaction Referral Ask + Logging
# Script for every touchpoint + tracking template
# Updated: April 2026

---

## The System

After every meaningful interaction — whether someone becomes a client, doesn't buy, or even just has a helpful conversation — ask for a referral. The ask is always low-pressure and always includes a specific reason why you're asking.

---

## When to Ask (Every Touchpoint)

### 1. After Winning a Deal (Day of signing)

"I'm really excited to work together. Quick question before we kick off: is there anyone in your network — another business owner, a friend, even your accountant or lawyer — who's mentioned needing a better website? I'd love an introduction. I'll take $100 off your first monthly invoice for every intro, whether or not they become a client."

### 2. After Delivering a Free Audit (Even if they don't buy)

"Glad the audit was helpful! Even if the timing isn't right for us to work together, do you know anyone else who could use a free website audit? I'm happy to record one for them too. Just send me their name and I'll reach out."

### 3. After a Sales Call That Doesn't Close

"I totally understand the timing isn't right. Before we wrap up — do you know anyone who IS actively looking for a new website or more leads? I'd love to help them, and you'd be doing them a favor by connecting us. No strings on your end."

### 4. After Launching a Client's Site

"Your site is live and I'm pumped about how it turned out. Now — you probably know other business owners who've complained about their website not working. Could you text or email one of them and introduce us? $100 off your next month for each intro."

### 5. In Every Monthly Report

Include this section at the bottom of every report:

"**Know someone who needs more leads from their website?** I have room for 2 new clients this month. Introductions are the highest compliment I can receive — and I'll credit $100 off your next month for each one. Just reply with their name or forward this email with a quick intro."

### 6. After a Client Hits a Milestone

"Congrats on hitting [X] leads this month! That's your best month yet. Quick favor — would you mind introducing me to one other business owner who could use results like these? Happy to do a free audit for anyone you connect me with."

### 7. After Solving a Support Issue

"Glad we got that sorted out. By the way — if you ever hear a fellow business owner frustrated with their website, I'd love to help. An introduction from you goes a long way. $100 off your next month as a thank you."

### 8. During Quarterly Check-Ins

"Things look great on your end. I have capacity for a couple more clients this quarter — is there anyone in your network who comes to mind? Fellow business owners, your vendors, your own clients even?"

### 9. After Someone Chooses a Competitor

"Totally respect your decision. One more thing — even though we're not working together, if you ever meet someone looking for a website that's focused on lead generation, I'd love an introduction. And if the new site doesn't work out, my door is always open."

### 10. During Casual Conversations (Networking, DMs)

"By the way, I'm always looking to connect with local business owners who want more leads from their website. If anyone comes to mind, even casually, a quick intro means a lot to me."

## Making the Ask Easy

Always remove friction for the referrer:

**Option A: Give them a copy-paste message**

"Here's something you can copy/paste if it's easier:

'Hey [Name], I want to connect you with Dan. He builds websites for local businesses that actually generate leads. He did mine and it's been great. If you're interested, his site is [URL] or you can reach him at [email]. Tell him I sent you.'"

**Option B: Offer to do the work**

"Just send me their name and email. I'll reach out and mention you introduced us. You don't have to do anything else."

**Option C: Give them your calendar link**

"Here's my calendar link — feel free to forward it to anyone: [Calendar link]. They can book a free 15-minute call without even going through you."

## Rules

1. **Ask at every touchpoint.** Not just with happy clients — everyone.
2. **Be specific.** "Do you know a dentist who needs a website?" is better than "Do you know anyone?"
3. **Make it reciprocal.** Offer to refer people to THEM too. "If anyone needs a [their service], you're my go-to recommendation."
4. **Credit promptly.** Apply the $100 credit as soon as the referral books a call. Send a thank-you message.
5. **Thank them regardless of outcome.** Even if the referral doesn't convert, thank the referrer warmly.
6. **Never stop asking.** The moment you stop asking is the moment referrals dry up.
